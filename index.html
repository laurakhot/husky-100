Welcome to My Husky 100 E-Portfolio

This portfolio showcases my journey as a University of Washington student, highlighting experiences that embody the Husky 100 criteria: leadership, innovation, connecting the dots, and making an impact.

Throughout my time at UW, I've worked to make a difference through teaching, building accessible technology, and empowering others. From mentoring students as a TA to creating web solutions that help local businesses and organizations, each experience has shaped my understanding of what it means to be a Husky.

My UW Journey

Fall 2022

Admitted as ENGRUD

Started my journey at UW as an Engineering Undeclared student, exploring different engineering paths and discovering my passion for technology.

Winter 2023

Took CSE 121

A pivotal class that changed everything. CSE 121 made me realize I love computing and problem-solving through code. Previously wanted to pursue bioengineering, but this course redirected my entire path.

Spring 2023

Declared ECE Major

Officially declared Electrical and Computer Engineering as my major, diving deeper into hardware and software systems.

Autumn 2023

Started TAing CSE 123

Began my journey as a Teaching Assistant, helping students learn programming fundamentals and discovering my passion for education and mentorship.

Winter 2024

Switched to Informatics

Made the decision to switch to Informatics (INFO), aligning my studies with my passion for human-centered technology, accessibility, and social impact.

Spring 2024

Became Web Impact President

Elected as President of Web Impact, leading a team of student developers to build technology solutions for non-profits and community organizations across Seattle.
