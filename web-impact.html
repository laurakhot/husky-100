Web Impact Projects

Through Web Impact, I've had the opportunity to lead development teams and create web solutions for real clients—non-profits, small businesses, and community organizations that need technology but lack the resources to build it themselves.

These projects taught me leadership, client communication, and how to navigate ambiguity while delivering solutions that make a tangible difference in people's lives.

Local Non-Profit Website Redesign

Fall 2023 • Team Lead

Led a team of 4 developers to redesign and rebuild a website for a Seattle-based non-profit serving underserved youth. Focused on accessibility, mobile responsiveness, and easy content management for non-technical staff.

Impact: The new website increased volunteer sign-ups by 40% and made it easier for families to access resources. The organization's director told us it transformed their ability to serve the community.

Leadership Lesson: I learned to balance technical excellence with client needs, delegate tasks based on team members' strengths, and communicate technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders. Leading this project taught me that the best solutions come from truly understanding the people you're serving.

Small Business E-Commerce Platform

Winter 2024 • Full-Stack Developer

Built an online store for a local artisan bakery, including inventory management, online ordering, and payment processing integration. Used React, Node.js, and Firebase.

Impact: Enabled the bakery to expand beyond in-person sales, reaching customers throughout the Seattle area. During our first month live, online orders accounted for 25% of their revenue.

Technical Growth: This project pushed me to learn full-stack development, handle real payment transactions securely, and optimize performance for mobile users. Working with real business constraints taught me to prioritize features and deliver MVP solutions quickly.

Community Event Management System

Spring 2024 • Technical Lead

Developed a custom event management platform for a community center to organize classes, workshops, and events. Features included registration, payment processing, and automated email confirmations.

Impact: Streamlined the registration process for hundreds of community members, reducing administrative work by 60% and making programs more accessible to low-income families through scholarship features.

Innovation: We built custom accessibility features including screen reader support and simplified forms for users with varying digital literacy levels. This project showed me how thoughtful design can remove barriers and expand access.

Environmental Advocacy Group Platform

Fall 2024 • Project Manager & Developer

Created an interactive platform for an environmental advocacy group to share data visualizations, educational resources, and organize volunteer activities. Integrated map-based features to highlight local environmental issues.

Impact: Helped the organization reach a wider audience with data-driven storytelling. The interactive maps and visualizations made complex environmental data accessible to the general public, leading to increased community engagement.

Connecting the Dots: This project combined my technical skills with my passion for environmental issues. I learned how technology can amplify important messages and mobilize communities around causes that matter.

Mentorship Program Portal

Winter 2025 • Lead Developer

Built a mentorship matching and management system for a program connecting UW students with professionals in their field. Includes profile creation, matching algorithm, and communication tools.

Impact: Connected over 100 students with mentors in their first quarter of operation. The platform made it easier to scale the program and track mentorship outcomes.

Personal Connection: Having benefited from mentorship myself, building this platform felt deeply meaningful. It's a reminder that the work we do as developers can create opportunities and change lives.

What I've Learned

Web Impact projects have taught me that great software engineering isn't just about writing clean code—it's about understanding real problems, collaborating effectively, and creating solutions that genuinely help people.

Through these experiences, I've developed:

  • Leadership skills: Managing teams, communicating with clients, and delivering projects on time
  • Technical versatility: Full-stack development, database design, API integration, and deployment
  • Problem-solving: Working with ambiguous requirements and adapting to changing needs
  • Empathy: Understanding users' needs and building accessible, inclusive solutions

These projects embody the Husky 100 values of making an impact, connecting skills across disciplines, and using innovation to serve communities.
