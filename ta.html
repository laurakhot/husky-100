Teaching & Mentorship

As a Teaching Assistant at the University of Washington, I've had the privilege of guiding hundreds of students through their journey in computer science. Each quarter brought new challenges, growth opportunities, and the chance to create spaces where struggling feels safe and asking questions feels welcome.

Click on each card to read my reflection on that quarter's experience.

Autumn 2024

CSE 123 - Intro to Programming III

First quarter as a TA, supporting students learning Java fundamentals and problem-solving basics.

Click to read reflection →

Reflection: My first quarter as a TA was both exhilarating and humbling. I quickly learned that explaining concepts I took for granted required breaking down my thinking into digestible steps. One student struggled with loops for weeks, and watching them finally grasp the concept reminded me why I fell in love with programming in the first place.

This experience taught me patience and the importance of meeting students where they are. I developed strategies for explaining abstract concepts using real-world analogies, which became a foundation for my teaching style.

Winter 2025

CSE 373 - Data Structures & Algorithms

Taught Python, pandas, data analysis, and testing—bridging programming fundamentals with real-world applications.

Click to read reflection →

Reflection: CSE 163 allowed me to help students see the practical applications of programming in data science and research. Students worked on projects analyzing real datasets, which made the material feel relevant and impactful.

I particularly enjoyed guiding students through debugging complex data pipeline errors. These moments taught me the importance of systematic problem-solving and resilience—skills that extend far beyond programming. Watching students develop these skills has been incredibly fulfilling.
