Teaching & Mentorship

As a Teaching Assistant at the University of Washington, I've had the privilege of guiding hundreds of students through their journey in computer science. Each quarter brought new challenges, growth opportunities, and the chance to make a lasting impact on students' lives.

Click on each card to read my reflection on that quarter's experience.

Autumn 2023

CSE 142 - Intro to Programming I

First quarter as a TA, supporting students learning Java fundamentals and problem-solving basics.

Click to read reflection →

Reflection: My first quarter as a TA was both exhilarating and humbling. I quickly learned that explaining concepts I took for granted required breaking down my thinking into digestible steps. One student struggled with loops for weeks, and watching them finally grasp the concept reminded me why I fell in love with programming in the first place.

This experience taught me patience and the importance of meeting students where they are. I developed strategies for explaining abstract concepts using real-world analogies, which became a foundation for my teaching style.

Winter 2024

CSE 143 - Intro to Programming II

Helped students master data structures, recursion, and object-oriented programming principles.

Click to read reflection →

Reflection: CSE 143 presented more complex challenges, both for students and for me as an instructor. Recursion was particularly difficult to teach—it's one of those topics that either clicks immediately or requires a complete shift in thinking.

I created visual diagrams and animated examples to help students visualize the call stack, which became popular study materials. This quarter reinforced the value of innovation in teaching—sometimes traditional explanations aren't enough, and you need to find creative ways to reach students.

Spring 2024

CSE 154 - Web Programming

Introduced students to HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and building interactive web applications.

Click to read reflection →

Reflection: Teaching web development was especially rewarding because students could immediately see the visual results of their work. I loved watching their excitement when they deployed their first website or created their first interactive feature.

I emphasized accessibility from day one, teaching students to build with all users in mind. Many students told me this perspective changed how they thought about technology. This quarter connected my passion for accessible design with my role as an educator.

Autumn 2024

CSE 142 - Intro to Programming I

Returned to CSE 142 with a year of teaching experience and refined pedagogical approaches.

Click to read reflection →

Reflection: Coming back to CSE 142 after teaching higher-level courses gave me new appreciation for the fundamentals. I brought lessons learned from teaching web development and data structures to make intro programming more engaging and relevant.

I also took on more leadership responsibilities, mentoring newer TAs and helping redesign lab activities. This quarter was about connecting the dots between my experiences across different courses to become a more well-rounded educator.

Winter 2025

CSE 163 - Intermediate Data Programming

Taught Python, pandas, data analysis, and testing—bridging programming fundamentals with real-world applications.

Click to read reflection →

Reflection: CSE 163 allowed me to help students see the practical applications of programming in data science and research. Students worked on projects analyzing real datasets, which made the material feel relevant and impactful.

I particularly enjoyed guiding students through debugging complex data pipeline errors. These moments taught me the importance of systematic problem-solving and resilience—skills that extend far beyond programming. Watching students develop these skills has been incredibly fulfilling.

Spring 2025

Continuing the Journey

Looking ahead to new opportunities for growth, mentorship, and making an impact through teaching.

Click to read reflection →

Reflection: Each quarter as a TA has reinforced my passion for education and my commitment to making computer science accessible to all students. I've learned that great teaching isn't about having all the answers—it's about guiding students to discover answers themselves.

Moving forward, I'm excited to continue mentoring, develop new teaching materials, and explore ways to make CS education more inclusive and engaging. The relationships I've built with students and the growth I've witnessed make this one of the most meaningful parts of my UW experience.

Impact & Growth

Over six quarters, I've supported over 500 students in their programming journey. Beyond teaching code, I've learned to foster growth mindsets, create inclusive learning environments, and adapt my approach to meet diverse learning needs.

This experience embodies the Husky 100 values of leadership (mentoring and guiding students), innovation (creating new teaching materials and approaches), and making an impact (helping students succeed and discover their potential).
